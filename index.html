Sprinter Van Side Steps vs Running Boards: Which Is Right for Your Build?

9 min read Updated June 2026 Exterior & Entry

A Mercedes Sprinter's side door sits roughly 24 inches off the ground — high enough that climbing in and out can feel like a daily workout, especially for kids, dogs, and anyone with tired knees after a long drive. The fix is a step. But should you bolt on a low-profile side step or a full-length running board? They look similar, they're often confused, and they solve the same problem in two very different ways.

This guide breaks down exactly how the two differ — in design, function, clearance, and price — so you can pick the right one for how you actually use your van.

The Short Answer

Choose a side step if you want a clean, "high-and-tight" look, maximum ground clearance for off-road and overland use, and the lightest, simplest entry aid. Choose a running board if you want a continuous step that runs the length of the door opening, easier entry at multiple points, and extra protection for your van's lower body panels. For most adventure Sprinters that see dirt roads, the low-profile side step is the better balance.

What is a side step?

A side step is a compact, bolt-on platform mounted directly beneath the sliding door. It gives you a single, solid foothold right where you need it — and nothing more. What sets a premium step like the Vanspeed Sprinter Side Step apart:

  • CNC-cut aircraft-grade aluminum — strong but light, won't rust
  • Powder-coated matte black with a textured, non-slip tread
  • Tucks high & tight to preserve break-over and departure angles off-road
  • True no-drill install using your van's factory mounting points

What is a running board?

A running board (sometimes called a nerf bar) is a longer step that spans most or all of the door opening — and on some designs, the full length of the van between the wheel wells. Instead of one foothold, you get a continuous platform you can step onto from anywhere. That length buys you a few things:

  • Step on from anywhere along the door — easier for a row of passengers
  • A natural foothold when loading the roof rack
  • Extra protection for the lower body panels from rocks and debris
  • The trade-off: boards hang lower and wider, giving up clearance on steep terrain

Watch your clearance

If your Sprinter is lifted or you spend real time on rutted forest roads and sand, clearance is everything. A low-slung running board is the first thing to scrape on a steep approach. This is the single biggest reason overlanders lean toward a tucked-up side step.

So which should you choose?

It comes down to where your van spends its time and who's climbing in and out of it.

⛰️
Pick a side step if…

You go off-pavement, run a lift kit, value clearance and a minimal look, or just want the lightest, cleanest single-step solution. This is the right call for most adventure builds.

👨‍👩‍👧
Pick a running board if…

Your van is mostly a street-driven family hauler, you load passengers, kids, and dogs constantly, and you want easy entry at any point plus extra body protection.

🪜
Consider both — front and rear — if…

You want easy cab entry and a stable rear step at the back doors. Many builders pair a side step at the slider with a separate rear ladder step.

Fitment & installation

Both options are designed to bolt on. The best side steps use your Sprinter's factory threaded mounting points, so there's no drilling and the panels stay watertight — most builders finish the job with hand tools in under an hour. Before you buy, confirm three things:

  • Wheelbase — 144" or 170"
  • Model year — 2007–2018 "NCV3" vs 2019+ "VS30"
  • Lift kit — running a lift changes the ideal step height

When in doubt, our team can confirm fitment for your exact van.

Frequently asked questions

Are side steps and running boards the same thing?

No. A side step is a compact platform mounted at a single point beneath the door, while a running board is a longer step that spans the length of the door opening or the body. People use the terms interchangeably, but they differ in length, clearance, and price.

Do I really need a step on my Sprinter van?

You don't strictly need one, but with the side door sitting around 24 inches off the ground, a step makes daily entry dramatically easier and safer — particularly for children, older passengers, and dogs. It's one of the most popular first upgrades for a reason.

Will a step hurt my off-road clearance?

A low-profile side step tucks tight to the body and preserves clearance, which is why it's preferred for overland and off-road builds. A full-length running board hangs lower and wider and is more likely to scrape on steep or rutted terrain.

Can I install a side step myself?

Yes. Quality side steps like the Vanspeed are designed as a no-drill, bolt-on install using factory mounting points, and ship with all required hardware. Most owners complete the job with basic hand tools.

Which is better for a family van?

For a street-driven family van with kids and pets getting in and out all day, a running board's continuous step and multiple entry points are hard to beat. For an adventure van that sees dirt, a side step is the smarter pick.
